Our Journey

From Personal Challenge to Continental Solution

Founded by Fiona Juma Ajowi, who faced epilepsy's challenges firsthand, Epicare Africa was born from a vision to bridge the treatment gap and stigma surrounding epilepsy across the continent.

What started as a personal mission has grown into a movement, empowering thousands with tools, education, and support to manage epilepsy with dignity and confidence.

Our Core Beliefs

Guiding principles that drive everything we do at Epicare Africa

Our Mission

To revolutionize epilepsy care in Africa through innovative technology, education, and community empowerment, ensuring every individual with epilepsy receives dignified and effective support.

  • Develop accessible epilepsy management tools
  • Combat stigma through education
  • Bridge the treatment gap across Africa

Our Vision

A future where epilepsy is no longer a barrier to living a full and productive life in Africa, where communities are educated, and quality care is accessible to all.

  • Africa leading in epilepsy innovation
  • Zero preventable epilepsy-related deaths
  • Thriving epilepsy-aware communities

Our Innovative Approach

Combining technology, education, and community engagement to transform epilepsy care

Digital Solutions

Mobile apps for seizure tracking, medication reminders, and emergency alerts designed for low-bandwidth environments.

Safety Products

Affordable seizure safety kits and protective gear tailored for African climates and living conditions.

Education

The "Epi Girl" animated series and school programs to educate children and combat stigma from an early age.

Our Journey

Key milestones in our mission to transform epilepsy care in Africa

2018 - The Beginning

Founded by Fiona Juma Ajowi after her personal experience with epilepsy, with a vision to create change.

2019 - First Product Launch

Released our first seizure tracking app, reaching 5,000 users in East Africa within the first year.

2021 - Epi Girl Launch

Introduced the Epi Girl educational series, now used in over 200 schools across 3 countries.

2023 - Regional Expansion

Expanded operations to West Africa, establishing partnerships with healthcare providers in Nigeria and Ghana.

2025 - 50,000 Lives Impacted

Reached our milestone of directly impacting over 50,000 individuals living with epilepsy across Africa.
